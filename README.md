# THF January Sales Performance Dashboard (Power BI)

## 📊 Project Overview

This project analyzes the January sales performance of **Tabitha House of Fragrance (THF)**, a retail perfume business.

The dashboard was developed to provide clear insights into revenue trends, product performance, customer behavior, and sales distribution to support data-driven business decisions.
Using Microsoft Power BI, raw sales records were transformed into meaningful visual insights to help the business evaluate profitability and improve operational strategy.

---

## 🎯 Business Objectives

The key objectives of this analysis were to:

- Determine total revenue generated in January  
- Identify top-selling products  
- Identify highest-purchasing customers  
- Evaluate overall product performance  
- Analyze sales distribution patterns  
- Calculate profit margins and profitability trends  
- Determine the percentage of new vs. returning customers  

---

## 🗂 Dataset Description

The dataset contains January sales transaction records with the following fields:

- Order ID  
- Sales Date  
- Product Name  
- Units Sold  
- Unit Price  
- Sales Price  
- Market Price  
- Transport Cost  
- Total Revenue  
- Total Cost Price (Market Price + Transport Cost)  
- PIB (Pay-In-Bit subscription status)  
- Product Type (In-store or not)  
- Returning Customer (New or Returning)  

### Data Cleaning & Preparation

The following preprocessing steps were performed:

- Standardized date formats  
- Created calculated fields (Revenue, Cost, Profit)  
- Removed inconsistencies  
- Validated numeric columns for accuracy  
- Ensured correct data categorization  

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies Used

- Microsoft Power BI – Data transformation, modeling, and visualization  
- Microsoft Excel – Initial data cleaning and structuring  

---

## 📈 Dashboard Features

The dashboard includes:

- KPI Cards (Total Revenue, Total Quantity Sold, Profit)  
- Top Performing Products (Bar Chart)  
- Product Performance Breakdown  
- Revenue Distribution Analysis  
- Customer Type Analysis (New vs Returning)  
- Pay-In-Bit Usage Overview  

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

From the analysis:

- A significant portion of sales came from returning customers, indicating strong customer retention.  
- Products available in-store generated higher profits due to lower transportation costs.  
- Mysterious Perfume Oil recorded the highest units sold, suggesting strong demand and the need for restocking.  
- The Pay-In-Bit option was used by four customers. While adoption is currently low, it provides flexible purchasing opportunities and could be expanded.  

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

Based on the findings:

- Increase inventory levels for top-performing products to avoid stockouts.  
- Promote in-store stocking strategy to reduce transportation costs and increase margins.  
- Create targeted marketing campaigns to convert new customers into returning buyers.  
- Increase awareness of the Pay-In-Bit option to improve customer flexibility and sales volume.  
- Conduct monthly performance reviews to monitor trends and adjust strategies accordingly.
